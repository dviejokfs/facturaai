# es-ES Creative Brief

> Single source of truth for Spanish (Spain) ad scripts. Update monthly from real customer interviews. This file is the moat — code is plumbing.

## Audience

Spanish self-employed (**autónomos**). Ages 25-55. Plumbers, electricians, designers, photographers, consultants, café owners, freelance developers. ~90% already have a **gestor**. Frustrated by the trimester scramble to find invoices in Gmail, WhatsApp, drawers, photo libraries.

They are not shopping for "fire your gestor" software. They are drowning in receipt chaos and want help looking organized when they hand things off.

## Vocabulary bank (use these words verbatim)

- **autónomo / autónoma** — never "freelancer" or "trabajador independiente"
- **trimestre, trimestral** — Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 in Spain are the rhythm of life
- **Hacienda** — never "el fisco" or "los impuestos" alone when "Hacienda" fits
- **gestor / gestora** — never "contable" (that's a different profession in Spain)
- **modelo 130, modelo 303, modelo 100, modelo 390** — real Spanish tax forms
- **cuota de autónomo** — the monthly social security payment they hate
- **factura, ticket, recibo** — three different things; respect the distinction
- **IVA soportado** (input VAT) / **IVA repercutido** (output VAT)
- **deducible** — the magic word; gastos deducibles
- **paralela** — what Hacienda sends when something doesn't match (terror)
- **alta, baja** — registering / deregistering as autónomo

## Phrases to AVOID (gringo Spanish / wrong register)

- ❌ "contador" — Mexican, sounds wrong in Spain
- ❌ "impuestos" used alone where "Hacienda" or "trimestre" fits
- ❌ "aplicación" — say **app**
- ❌ Over-formal **usted** — always **tutear** in ads
- ❌ Latin American expressions ("ahorita", "platicar", etc.)
- ❌ Direct translations of English UGC ("game changer", "literally", "vibe")

## Pain points (ranked by intensity, from interviews)

1. **"Busco facturas la noche antes del trimestre"** — frantic search through Gmail, WhatsApp, the drawer
2. **"Le mando un ZIP de 47 PDFs a mi gestora y me odia"** — handoff is chaotic
3. **"Seguro me olvido gastos deducibles"** — money left on the table
4. **"Apunto todo en Excel como si fuera 2005"** — primitive tooling
5. **"Mi gestora me cobra extra cuando todo está desordenado"** — disorganization tax
6. **"Mi pareja me echa la bronca cada trimestre"** — domestic friction is real
7. **"Tengo facturas en 4 sitios diferentes"** — Gmail, WhatsApp, Drive, fotos del móvil

## Positioning (non-negotiable)

> **InvoScanAI NO sustituye a tu gestor. Lo complementa.**
> Ordena tus facturas automáticamente para que el envío al gestor sea un clic, no un drama.

The gestor stays. InvoScanAI makes the autónomo a *good client* to their gestor. Possible future channel: gestores recommending it to their own clients.

## Forbidden angles

- ❌ "Despide a tu gestor"
- ❌ "Hazlo tú mismo, sin gestor"
- ❌ Comparaciones de precio con el gestor
- ❌ "AI que hace tus impuestos" — no, ordena facturas
- ❌ Cualquier cosa que pinte al gestor como enemigo

## Proven angles (update with winners after each test cycle)

- **Trimestre panic → calm** — el día 15, caos, hasta que…
- **Gestora POV** — "ojalá todos mis clientes usaran esto"
- **Gmail sync surprise** — "encontré 23 facturas que iba a olvidar"
- **Export hero shot** — antes: ZIP caótico / después: un PDF limpio
- **Pareja relief** — "este trimestre no me ha echado la bronca"
- **Modelo 303 panic** — "¿qué pongo en la casilla 30?" → "ya está todo aquí"

## Hook patterns that work

- "POV: eres autónomo y…"
- "Llevo X años siendo autónomo y…"
- "Nadie te dice que ser autónomo en España es…"
- "Si eres autónomo, esto te va a sonar:"
- "Mi gestora me dijo: 'por fin'"
- "El día 15 del trimestre, mi vida:"

## CTA

**"Descárgala gratis en el App Store. €7 al mes."**

Variants:

- "Pruébala gratis"
- "Tu gestor te lo va a agradecer"
